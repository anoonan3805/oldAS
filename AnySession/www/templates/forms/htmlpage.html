Weekly Newsletter

Friends,

History knows the names of a few select individuals who seek to push the limits of what's possible in order to improve their world, and the one future generations will inhabit down the line. Despite creating a monumental wave of change in the world, they challenge themselves not with an award in mind, but for the goal of a creating a better community for all. This week, we remember those individuals and a few more who have won the Nobel Prize.

Laureates have been numerous as they are diverse, from the original greats such as Albert Einstein and Martin Luther King Jr., to recent game-changers Nelson Mandela and Malala Yousafzai. Thanks to their genius and unwavering bravery, the world is never short on phenomenal scientists to cure the world, or human rights champions to lead it.

To all of this year's winners, congratulations; but more importantly, thank you.

Hispanic Congressional Caucus Institute

October 11 - 13

Internship Opportunity

The Hispanic Congressional Caucus Institute is looking for Latino Undergraduates to intern in Washington DC. - October 12, 9 AM, North Las Vegas Campus, Building S, Room 120

- October 13, 10 AM, Charleston Campus, Building I, Room 208
